Extracurricular Activities

One of the things I usually like to do is volunteering.

In my last year of highschool, I took up volunteering at Nehemyia Autistic Center and it was really fulfiling. It's a school for autistic children at a nursery level. It was pretty clear not a lot of people knew about it because they were clearly understaffed and in need of helpful volunteers.

There’s also another volunteer society called Care for Epilepsy which focuses on the condition epilepsy and how to create awareness about it. It was worth joining it since it helped bring a lot perspective about how dangerous epilepsy can be. I encourage people to check it out.

Some Inspiration

There are many people in the world that have truly amazing and inspiring stories. One that stuck with me is that of Malala Yousafzai's.

I read her book around the time where I was in middle school and it's safe to say that I was completely awed by her. Malala Yousafzai is a girl who refused to be denied the right to education by the Taliban, the people who were taking over her homeland Pakistan.

She became an advocate for girl's education when she was a child herself. Not only did she get shot in the head by a Taliban gunsman and survived, she was also the youngest person to receive a Nobel Peace Prize.

She's an amazing example for people who want to fight for what they believe in and make the world a better place. I think people can learn a lot from her and I strongly encourage reading her book.
